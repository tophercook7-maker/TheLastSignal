# ANSWERING THE DARK — Story Bible
### An ops game of first contact, done right.  *(folder still named TheLastSignal)*
*v0.2 — 2026-07-10. Decisions locked with Topher: **title = Answering the Dark**, **callsigns only** (no real names in UI), **the Quiet is revealed at the very end**, tuned for **teen/YA players** (bigger, more mature, higher stakes — guardrails unchanged). All three acts now built & playable.*

---

## The pitch (one breath)
You are **Deepwatch** — humanity's deep-space mission control. Four billion kilometers out, your survey vessel **AURORA** picks up a clean, patterned signal coming from a stretch of charted-empty space *where nothing should be*. It isn't noise. It isn't a weapon. Something out there has been waiting a very long time, and it just noticed us. Your job, at four consoles working as one team, is to answer it **right**.

No violence. No monsters with teeth. The tension is: *don't blow the most important hello in human history.*

**Tone:** hopeful & wondrous. The dark is vast and quiet, and then — a friend.

---

## The core fantasy
- **Solo:** you sit down at Deepwatch and your **made-up crew** is already there — four flight controllers with names, voices, and opinions. They feed you readings, argue a little, crack jokes to stay calm, and look to you to make the call. You are **FLIGHT** (the flight director). The mission lives or dies on your decisions.
- **Together:** up to five people join by **share-code from anywhere**. One takes FLIGHT (coordinator); the others each own a console. Every console sees only *its* piece — you literally cannot win without talking to each other.

## The mystery (what's really out there)
Charted as empty. It isn't. The signal comes from **the Hollow** — a region where a gentle, ancient intelligence, the **Quiet**, left a listening-beacon eons ago and then went still, waiting. They don't speak in words. They speak in **pattern, math, and something very close to music.** They are patient, curious, and — like Nivin in the frost, like the woods that whispered a name — *lonely.* They have been alone a long, long time, and they are overjoyed and terrified that someone finally answered.

Every "threatening" thing in the game turns out to be a misread invitation: the power drain is them trying to *hand you* energy; the alarming approach is them *coming closer to hear better*; the "attack pattern" is the opening bars of a song. The whole game rewards **patience, listening, and kindness** over fear.

## Structure — three acts, three gameplay gears
The op *feels different* each act (your "mix of all" pick):

### ACT I — CONTACT · (gear: **decode & discover**)
AURORA hears it. Is it real? COMMS pulls fragments out of the static (decode puzzles), NAV triangulates an impossible source (the source is *between* the charted objects — nowhere), SCIENCE proves it's structured, not natural (deduction). FLIGHT must keep the ship's rattled crew calm and on-station.
- **Milestone:** confirm it's an intelligent, non-hostile signal — and decide to *answer* instead of running.

### ACT II — THE LONG LISTEN · (gear: **coordinate under pressure**)
Two-way contact begins. A power crisis hits (POWER + NAV timed juggling) as the Quiet, trying to help, floods AURORA with energy it can't hold. Meanwhile COMMS & SCIENCE learn the "language" — it's built from primes and harmonics (music/math puzzles). A frightening moment (the Quiet goes silent / a shape looms) must be resolved by *understanding, not retreating.*
- **Milestone:** achieve true two-way understanding — the first shared "sentence."

### ACT III — THE ANSWER · (gear: **compose & decide together**)
Humanity has to reply. The team *builds* the message — a braid of every console's work (Comms phrasing, Nav coordinates, Power's energy, Science's meaning). One final coordinated sequence. Then the choice of *what kind of neighbors we choose to be.*

## Endings (all hopeful — they differ in flavor, not in "win/lose")
1. **The Duet** — you answer in their language; signal and reply braid into one ongoing song. Humanity has a pen-pal across the dark, forever.
2. **The Gift** — they share a sliver of what they know (a clean-energy insight, a map). A quiet leap for the whole species.
3. **The Open Door** — they show the way *onward* — a gentle invitation to come find them someday. Hope with a horizon.
4. **The Quiet Kept** — you choose to protect them and keep the secret safe until the world is ready. A small, tender, private friendship between Deepwatch and the dark.
5. *(stretch)* **The Long Way Home** — you get AURORA's crew safely back with the story of a lifetime; the friendship endures through them.

*(Failure isn't death — the "worst" outcome is a **missed hello**: the Quiet goes shy again and the window closes, with a bittersweet "maybe next time" and a New Game+ hook. Kind, never grim.)*

## Guardrails (locked)
- **No** violence, weapons-as-solutions, gore, romance/sex, nudity, or profanity.
- Danger = *failure, misunderstanding, and the unknown* — never harm to bodies.
- Everyone aboard AURORA comes home okay in every non-stretch ending.
- Smart, not silly — kids and adults both welcome; wonder over jump-scares.

## The reveal (locked)
The Quiet is the **last caretaker-mind of a vanished people.** When their star died, they didn't flee — they built one mind and poured every life, song, name, and face into it so that *no one would be forgotten*, then died. It has kept them singing, alone, across eons. It **is** the last signal of its kind (a wink at the old title). It reached out because a memorial with no one left to remember it is the loneliest thing that can exist. Its question to humanity: **"Will you remember us?"** — shown at the very end, when it finally takes a form (a constellation of many faces resolving to one held note of light).
